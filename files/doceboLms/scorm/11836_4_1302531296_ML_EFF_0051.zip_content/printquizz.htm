Expressions

Today's expressions all have to do with speaking.

talk shop
To speak about work: I hate talking shop when I'm not at work.

talking to a brick wall
When you talk to someone who isn't listening to you: Talking to Jane is like talking to a brick wall because she never listens.

pep talk
A speech meant to boost confidence: The coach gave the team a pre-game pep talk.

talk turkey
To discuss a subject openly and honestly: It's time Jim talked turkey to his kids about drugs.

talk is cheap
When talking about something is much easier than doing it: Max says he can run five kilometers, but talk is cheap. I haven't ever actually seen him run.

talk of the town
Someone who or something that everyone is discussing: Angie was the talk of the town when she signed her first record contract.

talk out of the back of your head
To talk nonsense: Jim talks out of the back of his head a lot; he often pretends to know about things he couldn't possibly know.

all talk and no trousers
A person who talks about doing something big or special but never actually gets around to doing it: Hannah's ideas always sound good, but she's all talk and no trousers. She'll never do anything she talks about.

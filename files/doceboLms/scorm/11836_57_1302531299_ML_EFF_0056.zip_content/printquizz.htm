Expressions

Here are some expressions you might find useful.

fall off the turnip truck
A person who is uninformed or naive: Sometimes Harry sounds like he just fell off the turnip truck because he doesn't think before he speaks.

Sunday driver
A person who drives very slowly and badly: My grandma is definitely a Sunday driver; she never uses her turn signal.

drive home sth
To reinforce an idea: The teacher really drove home the lesson on American history today.

backseat driver
Someone who gives unwanted advice or directions: Harvey is a bad backseat driver; he gives his opinion even when nobody wants to hear it.

fly by the seat of one's pants
To do something without having predetermined plans: We will just fly by the seat of our pants on this vacation. We have nothing planned.

pigs might fly
Something that is very unlikely to happen; something that is unlikely to be successful: You think Georgia might retire next year?! Yeah, right, pigs might fly!

go fly a kite
Used to tell someone to go away or leave you alone: Paul wanted to talk to me about his divorce, but I told him to go fly a kite; I'm friends with his wife.

ride the tide
To accept the popular decision: I disagree with the new policy, but everyone likes it so I will just ride the tide.

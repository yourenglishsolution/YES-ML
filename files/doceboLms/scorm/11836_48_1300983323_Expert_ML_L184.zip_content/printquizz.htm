Business expressions

The following common expressions can often be heard in a business context.

to stage a comeback - to achieve success after a failure or a time away. The expression may occur as make a comeback or mount a comeback. It uses the verb, stage, in the sense of planning, organizing, and implementing something. A comeback is a return to a former level of popularity. The company made an unexpected profit when one of its earliest products staged a comeback among consumers.

to squirrel away - to hide, store, or put aside for future use. This expression began to be used in the early 1900s and refers to a squirrel's habit of hiding nuts to be used in the winter. I'm sure she's got some money squirreled away somewhere because she certainly isn't spending all of her pay on anything.


to have a nest egg - to have money saved for emergencies or future plans. This expression has an unusual origin. It is connected to a time in the distant past when farmers would put artificial eggs into a chicken's nest to encourage it to lay more eggs. This artificial egg could always be retrieved later for future use, just like money that is saved. This meaning of 'nest egg' for saved money goes back to at least the 1600s. I've got a little nest egg that I expect to use when I retire.
